Emotion in Art

Explore the emotions we can get from arts and how they influence our favor of different art styles.

WikiArt Emotions Dataset has annotastion for around 4000 pieces of arts created between 1415 and 2012 from 4 western styles: Renaissance Art, Post Renaissance Art, Modern Art, and Contemporary Art. The four styles of arts consist of 22 categories: Early Renaissance, High Renaissance, Northern Renaissance, Baroque, Neoclassicism, Realism, Rococo, Romanticism, Abstract Art, Abstract Expressionism, Art Informel, Color Field Painting, Cubism, Expressionism, Impressionism, Lyrical Abstraction, Magic Realism, Neo-Expressionism, Pop Art, Post-Impressionism, Surrealism, and Minimalism.

Art pieces in the dataset are annotated with 20 emotions: Gratitude, Happiness, Humility, Love, Optimism, Trust, Anger, Arrogance, Disgust, Fear, Pessimism, Regret, Sadness, Shame, Agreeableness, Anticipation, Disagreeableness, Shyness, Surprise, and Neutral from 3 types: Positive, Negative, and Other or Mixed.

What art Style Categories do people like?

The style and category of art that dominates art creation has been changing over the years. The past one and a half century has witnessed the booming of Modern Art with miscellaneous categories as well as the emerge of Minimalism as Contemporary Art, a much more diversified situation than the four and a half centuries of Renaissance Art and Post Renaissance Art before that.

However, the popularity of art creation does not necessarily result in the popularity of the art style with observers. People nowadays like most art pieces of Renaissance and Post Renaissance art styles, a taste which they don't always have for Modern and Contemporary Arts.

Styles & Categories

Each art style has different numbers of categories, and the number of annotated arts in WikiArt Emotions Dataset is generally the same for each category. As a result, we see the most annotated art pieces for Modern Art, which has the largest number of categories, followed by Post Renaissance Art, Renaissance Art, and lastly Contemporary Art.

Like Rating

on a scale from -3 (dislike it a lot) to 3 (like it a lot)

People today tend to like arts of Renaissance and Post Renaissance Arts, but their attitudes towards Modern Arts vary a lot by the specific category. Contemporary Art is not really favored by contemporary people.

What emotions does art evoke in observers?

Art evokes emotions in observers. One piece of art can arouse multiple types of emotions, and different art can arouse different emotions. Is there any pattern in the emotions art pieces of different styles and categories evoke?

Emotions & Types

Most art pieces evoke Positive emotions, especially Happiness, Trust , Humility , and Optimism. Less art pieces evoke Negative emotions. For the Negative emotions people feel, Sadness, Disgust, and Fear are more common. Other or Mixed emotions are least evoked, for which Anticipation and Surprise are the most common.

Emotion Rating

on a scale from 0 (no people feel the emotion) to 1 (100% of people feel the emotion)

Considering all Styles and Categories, Happiness, Trust, Humility and Optimism are the most common Positive emotions people feel about arts. Fear and Sadness are the most commonly evoked Negative emotions. Surprise and Anticipation are the most commonly aroused Other or Mixed emotions.

What is the relation between the evoked emotions and people's preference?

Is there any relationship between the degree people like an art Style and the type and degree of Emotions that art Style evokes? Very likely! People tend to like arts evoking strong Positive emotions more.

Renaissance and Post Renaissance Arts are the two art Styles people like most. They both evoke the strongest Positive emotions and weakest Other or Mixed emotions. The Negative emotions they evoke are also relatively strong compared to the emotions evoked by Modern and Contemporary Arts.

Contemporary Art is the art Style people dislike. The emotions it arouses tend to be of a lower degree. The strongest emotion type it evokes is Other or Mixed and the weakest emotion type it evokes is Positive and Negative, a pattern opposite to Renaissance and Post Renaissance Arts.

People tend to like Modern Art, though to a much lower degree than Renaissance and Post Renaissance Arts. Modern Art evokes less strong emotions on average, though it still evokes stronger Positive emotions than Other or Mixed and Negative.
